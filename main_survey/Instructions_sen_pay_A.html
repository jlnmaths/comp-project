You can earn between $1 and $3 in this experiment.

Your payoff is calculated as follows: First, we randomly select one of the three rounds you played. You are then assigned to another participant of the experiment who plays the role of receiver.

Your assigned receiver sees the table and hint you have chosen in the selected round and is asked to assess with which probability there is a 1 behind the question mark (?). The higher this stated probability is, the higher your payoff.

The receiver is payed in such a way that it is optimal for them to state their true assessment of the probability.

Exact calculation of your payoff:
Your assigned receiver states a number between 0 and 100 which we call p. This p represents the probability in % with which they believe there is a 1 hidden behind the question mark (?). You then receive a payment of $ 1 + p/100*2. For instance, if your assigned receiver states a probability of 50%, you obtain a total payment of $2.
